Jan. 27, 2000

DENVER, CO - The Raptors shooting woes continued Thursday as they turned in their worst performance of the season in a 51-29 rout by the Denver Nuggets. While the Raptors had their poorest shooting game of the season, shooting just 27 percent from the field, the Nuggets managed to shoot 54 percent, including 7-of-10 from downtown. George McCloud led the Nuggets with 18 points including 4-of-5 three-pointers.

McCloud led the Nuggets three-point
attack going 4-for-5 from downtown

Coach Mike Aparicio had few words after the disappointing loss. "Our guys got killed tonight," said Aparicio. "Even if we shot our best game tonight, we still might have lost. The Nuggets were hitting everything. We really have to work on our defense. The offense will come, but we have to stop our opponents, and we're just not doing that yet."

Dell Curry led the Raptors with nine points, but shot just 3-for-12 from the field. The Raptors were just 4-of-24 from the field in the first half, and trailed 24-11 at the break. They made a brief run in the third, but the Nuggets continued to extend the lead with their incredible three-point shooting. By the fourth quarter, it seemed the Raptors had lost all will to continue.

"That was a really tough loss to swallow," said Vince Carter, who was held to 2-of-6 shooting. "We really stunk up the place tonight. It's easy to make excuses, but we're not going to do that. We're just going to keep working hard. The wins will come eventually."

The Raptors had no time to dwell on the loss, with another game just a few hours later in Utah. The Raptors are hoping to fine tune their game against a struggling Jazz team.
