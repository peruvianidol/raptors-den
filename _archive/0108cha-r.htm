Jan. 8, 2000

TORONTO, ONTARIO - Antonio Davis had a season-high 15 points to help the Raptors snap a two-game skid with a 48-36 win over the CPU-controlled Charlotte Hornets. The Raptors held the Hornets to 1-for-10 shooting in the second quarter, and led 23-13 at the half. They never trailed in the second half, as Vince Carter poured in 11 of his 20 points and shot 9-for-12 from the field. Carter also had four rebounds and four assists, earning him the Player of the Game award.

Antonio Davis had a

"I think I've been playing well so far this season," said Davis, who was a perfect 5-for-5 from the free-throw line. "But there's always room for improvement. I was feeling really good out there tonight, and I'm glad I could help us get a win."

Dell Curry, in his second start of the season, shot just 1-for-6 from the field, but locked up Eddie Jones defensively. Curry had four blocked shots against Jones, and held the Hornets top scorer to just eight points on 3-for-15 shooting. Bobby Phills led the Hornets with 11 points, and Elden Campbell had 10, but Charlotte shot just 30 percent from the field.

"We played a good game," said coach Mike Aparicio. "It was a nice team effort. Our bench played some quality minutes, really for the first time this season. Our defense was excellent - very consistent throughout the game. We hit all of our free throws which really helped us out. Hopefully we can keep our momentum going."

Doug Christie and Alvin Williams combined for five points off the bench for the Raptors. Dee Brown's shooting woes continued as he hit just 1-of-5 from the field. The win was the first home win for the Raptors, and the first game against a Central division opponent. The Raptors are off until Tuesday when they visit the Detroit Pistons.
